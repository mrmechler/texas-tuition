The Process

Four Steps to
In-State Tuition

Texas has clear residency requirements. With the right property and proper documentation, the path to in-state tuition is straightforward.

1

Purchase or Lease a Texas Property

Establish a domicile in Texas by purchasing or leasing a residential property. Owning property near UT Austin gives you a permanent Texas address and builds equity.

2

Live in Texas for 12 Months

Texas requires a continuous 12-month residency immediately before enrollment. Your student must live at the Texas address, not merely own or rent it.

3

Establish Domicile Intent

Get a Texas driver's license, register your vehicle, register to vote, and file taxes as a Texas resident. These actions demonstrate intent to make Texas your permanent home.

4

Apply for Reclassification

Submit a residency reclassification application to your Texas university. Once approved, your tuition drops to in-state rates — saving you up to $29,500 per year.

Requirements

Texas Residency
Requirements

Under Texas Education Code §54.052, these are the primary criteria the state uses to determine residency classification for tuition purposes.

📍

12-Month Physical Presence

You must reside continuously in Texas for the 12 months immediately preceding the census date of the semester in which you enroll.

🏠

Established Domicile

Owning or leasing a home in Texas and residing in it is one of the strongest indicators of domicile intent recognized by Texas universities.

🪪

Texas Driver's License

Obtaining a Texas DL within 30 days of establishing residency, and surrendering your prior state's license, is a key documentation requirement.

🗳️

Voter Registration

Registering to vote in Texas demonstrates your intent to make Texas your permanent home — a factor weighed heavily in residency determinations.

💼

Texas Tax Filing

Filing state and federal taxes as a Texas resident (or with a Texas address) provides official documentation of your domicile intent.

🚗

Vehicle Registration

Transferring your vehicle registration to Texas within 30 days of establishing residence further supports your residency classification application.
